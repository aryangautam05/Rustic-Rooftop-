Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is deep pruning and why is it important?

Deep pruning involves selectively removing larger branches or structural parts of the tree to improve health, light penetration, and growth patterns. It prevents disease and helps trees develop stronger structures.

How often should soil be tested for tree health?

We recommend soil testing at least every 2-3 years or if you notice changes in tree vigor or growth. Regular testing helps adjust care practices to maintain optimal soil conditions.

Tree Care Tips & Best Practices

Maintaining healthy trees requires understanding their biology and environment. Our expert tips ensure your trees get proper hydration, nutrition, and protection throughout the seasons.
  • Watering Mature Trees: Water deeply and less frequently. Deep watering encourages roots to grow downwards, increasing drought resistance. Light, frequent watering leads to shallow roots vulnerable to stress.
  • Seasonal Pruning: Prune during the late winter or early spring dormant periods to reduce stress and allow fresh growth to come in strong during growing season.
  • Organic Mulching: Apply organic mulch 3-4 inches thick around the base but avoid piling against the trunk. Mulch retains moisture, controls weeds, and slowly releases nutrients.
  • Pest and Disease Monitoring: Regularly inspect leaves and bark for symptoms. Early detection and treatment prevent severe infestations.
  • Prevent Soil Compaction: Keep foot and vehicle traffic away from root zones as compacted soil inhibits root respiration and nutrient uptake.
  • Professional Support: For major interventions such as deep root fertilization or corrective pruning, trust certified arborists
  • Balanced Fertilization: Avoid overuse of fertilizer that can lead to weak growth and pollution. Test soil first to customize nutrient input.
  • Protection for Young Trees: Stake young trees loosely with breathable guards to shield from wind or animal damage without obstructing growth.
